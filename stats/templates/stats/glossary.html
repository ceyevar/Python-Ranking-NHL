Term Glossary and Explanation

GP

Games Played

PIM

Penalty Minutes

Denotes time the player has spent in the penalty box

PP and ES

Powerplay and Even Strength Designations

Powerplay stat categories denote stats accumulated while on the powerplay (which is when a team plays a man up). This is generally less valuable as an evaluation statistic because not all teams are given the same amount of powerplay time and not all players are given powerplay time.

1A

Primary Assist

In hockey, assists are credited to the direct passer and the player who passed to the direct passer. Primary assists are therefore the assists that result directly in a goal.

2A

Secondary Assist

In hockey, assists are credited to the direct passer and the player who passed to the direct passer. Secondary assists are therefore the assists that result in a pass that results in a goal. Secondary assists are disregarded in our algorithm because they contain little statistical value.

Goals Created

Compares a player's offensive contribution to his team

Calculated by adding goals scored to 0.5 times assists, then multiplying by team goals divided by team goals plus 0.5 times team assists.

Shooting Percentage

Goals scored/Shots taken

Any shooting percentage over the league norm is generally considering to be an indication of luck because goals themselves are low percentage events. Having a high shooting percentage can indicate a "sniper" player or someone who is riding a hot streak.

PDO

Measures a team's perceived luck

Is the sum of a team's save percentage and shooting percentage. Anything above 100 indicates a team is "lucky" and anything below 100 indicates a a team is "unlucky". Overtime all teams regress to the mean (100).

SV%

Save percentage

Goals scored divided by shots taken

SF, SA and SF%

Shots for, Shots Against, and Shots For Percentage

SF% is 100*SF/(SF+SA)
